# Laser Surface Micromachining‐Based PVC Diffusion Layer for Multimodal Wearable Sports Sensors

## 摘要与研究价值

**Original:** ABSTRACT Multimodal wearable sports sensors capable of simultaneously detecting both physical and biochemical signals represent a transformative approach to comprehensive physiological monitoring during exercise. However, most existing systems either focus solely on physical parameters or suffer from limited sensitivity and sensing range when targeting high‐concentration biochemical markers. There remains a challenge for integrated platforms that can robustly monitor metabolic status in realistic sports scenarios. This work presents M‐SportSense, an integrated wearable platform for real‐time multimodal sweat analysis that combines lactate and pH sensing. The core innovation is a laser surface micromachined PVC diffusion layer that dramatically enhances lactate detection performance. By employing a 1064 nm laser to create a circular hole array on the honeycomb PVC surface, the detection range is expanded by 100 times while simultaneously increasing sensitivity by 127%. The M‐SportSense integrates laser‐induced graphene (LIG) electrodes functionalized with lactate oxidase for lactate sensing, polyaniline for pH sensing, and serpentine LIG for temperature monitoring. A three‐layer microfluidic device with optimized inlet design ensures efficient sweat collection and rapid refreshing rates. On‐body validation during 20‐min indoor cycling demonstrates stable multimodal detection of sweat lactate and pH, validating the platform's potential for personalized exercise monitoring, fatigue assessment, and real‐time training optimization.
