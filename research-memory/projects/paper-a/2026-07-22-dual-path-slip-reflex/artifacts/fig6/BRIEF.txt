FIGURE 6 BRIEF - HOOK-LOAD GRASP REFLEX AND EVENT-LOCATION ENERGY AUDIT

One-sentence question under test
Does pre-ADC event formation reduce idle embedded-ADC conversions and ADC/reference
energy relative to a matched ADC-first event baseline while preserving a
preregistered grasping-performance boundary, and what is its net effect on MCU duty,
communication, and total electronics energy per task?

Allowed source slides
1, 20, 22, 25. Treat slide 25 as preliminary and definition-sensitive. The separate
experiment-planning schematic is analytic planning material, not measured evidence.

Layout
Use a 2 x 3 grid, approximately 87.5 mm wide x 39.3 mm high per panel, with
3 mm horizontal/vertical gutters and 4 mm margins. Preserve room for time-series axes.

Panel contract
a. Real gripper/sensor/electronics setup. Separate deployment tactile electronics,
   external continuous diagnostic instruments, motor-controller electronics, and
   actuator energy. Mark ADC/MCU P10/P12 and the planned overload path as TBC.
b. Frozen hook-load task with two GPIO event cycles: CONTACT edge -> wake/snapshot ->
   immediate stop/hold command -> optional burst/log; then X/Y load edge ->
   wake/snapshot -> one frozen bounded-action/burst policy -> grip/log/recovery.
c. Same-trial frames of approach, CONTACT stop, calibrated hook load, and bounded grip
   response/recovery. A one-dimensional gripper supports timed grip increase, not
   signed x-y correction.
d. Same-clock slots for load reference, Vz/Vx/Vy, CONTACT/X/Y events, embedded-ADC
   state, MCU_ACTIVE/commands, motor-controller state, and object slip.
e. Trial-level performance and a fair Vz-only ablation. Freeze the strongest
   calibration-derived Vz absolute/change trigger and use the same post-event action.
f. A1 raw-4 continuous, A2 vector-3 continuous, A3 matched ADC-first event, and A4
   matched pre-ADC event. A1-vs-A2 isolates vectorization; A3-vs-A4 isolates event
   location. Report conversions/data, measured MCU duty, communication, ADC/reference
   energy, total electronics energy per task, per-rail energy, and event-rate break-even.

Architecture and claim boundaries
- A3 and A4 share the same vector AFE, Vz/Vx/Vy ADC inputs, ADC/reference, active
  sample rate, physical thresholds, and downstream controller. A3 is not raw-4.
- A3 may already reduce MCU-main duty and communication. A4-specific hypothesized
  benefit is avoiding idle quantization; net MCU, communication, and total-energy
  effects must be measured.
- Write `standby to be verified` until P10 is locked and datasheet-defined standby or
  power-down is confirmed with ADC/reference rail current. ADC_EN/CS/DRDY alone is
  insufficient proof of internal converter/reference state.
- If A4 still converts continuously, downgrade to `hardware-thresholded event-driven
  control`; do not claim ADC-gated/event-triggered acquisition or reduced conversions.
- The overload hardware-disable path is planned and `[TO BE VALIDATED]` until P13/P14
  polarity, power-up/down safety, fault injection, and overload-to-disable latency pass.
- The 160-trial three-day set is a descriptive pilot. Formal non-inferiority sample
  size must be recalculated from pilot baseline rate, frozen margin, alpha, and power.
- Do not present slide-25 values as final without audited states, rails, task window,
  sampling configuration, and actuator exclusion.
