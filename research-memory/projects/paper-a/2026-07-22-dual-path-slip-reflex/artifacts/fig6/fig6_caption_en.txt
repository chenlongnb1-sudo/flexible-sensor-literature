Planned validation of hook-load grasp reflexes and the location of event formation. a, Existing source hardware provides the analog vector board, gripper context, and a preliminary power-monitor record; a real instrumented setup must separate deployment tactile electronics, external diagnostic acquisition, motor-controller electronics, and actuator power. b, Required frozen protocol with two GPIO event cycles: CONTACT wake and immediate stop/hold command followed by optional capture, then hook-load X/Y wake and one preregistered bounded-action/burst policy. c, Required same-trial frames of approach, contact stop, calibrated hook loading, bounded grip response, and recovery. d, Required same-clock records of load reference, Vz, Vx, Vy, CONTACT/X/Y events, embedded-ADC state, MCU_ACTIVE and commands, motor-controller state, and object slip. e, Required trial-level performance and fair Vz-only ablation using the strongest calibration-frozen Vz absolute/change trigger and the same post-event action. f, Required A1 raw-4 continuous versus A2 vector-3 continuous comparison, and matched A3 ADC-first versus A4 pre-ADC event comparison. A3 and A4 must share the vector AFE, Vz/Vx/Vy ADC inputs, ADC/reference, active sampling, physical thresholds, and controller. A3 may already gate MCU-main activity and communication; A4 specifically tests whether avoiding idle quantization reduces ADC/reference energy and how this affects total electronics energy per task. Embedded-ADC standby and the planned overload hardware-disable path remain to be validated. No performance or energy result is asserted before measurement.
